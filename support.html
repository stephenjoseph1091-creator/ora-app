Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Does Ora require an internet connection?
A: No! Ora is completely offline-first. All liturgical data is bundled with the app. Internet is never required.
Q: Which calendar does Ora follow?
A: Ora includes both the Traditional Latin Mass (1962 Missale) and Novus Ordo calendars. You can choose your preference in Settings.
Q: Can I sync liturgical events to my iOS Calendar?
A: Yes! Enable "Sync to Calendar" in Settings. Ora will create a calendar called "Catholic — Liturgical" with saints, holy days, and major feasts.
Q: Is my data private?
A: Absolutely. Ora collects NO data. Everything is stored locally on your device. See our Privacy Policy.
Q: Why doesn't every day have a saint?
A: The liturgical calendar doesn't assign a major saint feast to every day. Some days are "ferial" (ordinary days) in the liturgical season.
Q: Can I use Ora for both forms of the Mass?
A: Yes! Ora provides readings for both the Traditional Latin Mass (Extraordinary Form) and the Novus Ordo (Ordinary Form).
Q: Is Latin available?
A: Latin texts are coming in a future update. Currently, English is supported.
Q: How accurate is the liturgical data?
A: Ora uses authoritative sources: Divinum Officium for traditional texts and romcal for calendar calculations. All data has been verified against official sources.
Q: Can I request a feature?
A: Yes! Email us with your suggestion. We're especially interested in features that help people pray better.

Troubleshooting

Issue: Calendar sync isn't working
Solution:
  1. Go to iOS Settings → Ora → Enable "Calendars"
  2. Open Ora → Settings → Enable "Sync to Calendar"
  3. Return to iOS Calendar app and check for "Catholic — Liturgical" calendar
Issue: Notifications not appearing
Solution:
  1. Go to iOS Settings → Ora → Notifications → Enable all options
  2. Open Ora → Settings → Enable prayer reminders
  3. Choose your preferred times
Issue: App is showing wrong date
Solution: The app syncs with your device's date. Check iOS Settings → General → Date & Time. The app updates automatically at midnight.
Issue: Text is too small/large
Solution: Ora supports Dynamic Type. Adjust text size in iOS Settings → Display & Brightness → Text Size.

About the Data Sources

Ora uses open-source liturgical content:

  • Divinum Officium - Traditional Roman Breviary and Missale texts (MIT License)
  • romcal - Liturgical calendar computation (MIT License)
  • Butler's Lives of the Saints - Saint biographies (Public Domain)
